HR-PROC-001 — Recruitment & Hiring

From headcount request through sourcing, interviews, reference check, health screening, and offer. References Staff Requisition Form (CKP-HR-SRF-001) and Role Persona Cards (CKP-HR-RPC-001).

Owner: Yegi Approver: Caroll (SRF) + Jeremy (final hire) Target TAT: 30–45 days from SRF to joining Channels: Portals + Agencies + Social Media Agencies: Intellipro, Career Horizons

Decision Point
System / External Party
HOD
STEP 1
Identify Headcount Need
👤

HOD identifies staffing gap — new role or backfill. Documents: role title, department, reporting line, reason, urgency, and proposed start date.

⚠ Must use SRF (CKP-HR-SRF-001) — verbal requests not accepted.

Trigger: Anytime. Target: SRF submitted within 2 working days of decision.
→
STEP 2
Complete & Submit SRF
📋

HOD completes Staff Requisition Form (CKP-HR-SRF-001): all sections including Section E1 (Standard Persona Baseline) and E2 (Role-Specific Persona, referencing RPC-001). Submits to Yegi via Lark.

Incomplete SRFs returned to HOD within 1 working day. No sourcing begins until SRF is complete and approved.

↓
Caroll (GM)
STEP 3
SRF Review & Approval
✅

Caroll reviews SRF: justification, headcount budget, salary range, reporting structure, and priority level. Approves or returns with comments.

If approved: Yegi proceeds to sourcing immediately.
If rejected: HOD notified with reason. SRF revised and resubmitted if role still needed.

Decision within 2 working days of receipt.
↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 4A
Post on Job Portals
🖥️

Yegi prepares job description based on approved SRF and Persona Cards, then posts on all active portals simultaneously:

Portals: A Job Thing, Indeed, JobStreet, CKP Website careers page.

Ad copy must include: role title, key responsibilities, required background (outsourcing/agency mandatory), salary range (optional), and application instructions.

Candidates without outsourcing/agency background — auto-rejected at screening stage. Do not proceed regardless of other qualifications.

All portal ads live within 3 working days of SRF approval.
→
STEP 4B
Post on Social Media
📲

Yegi coordinates with BD/marketing to publish job posts on CKP's social media channels: Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn.

LinkedIn post targets professionals directly — include role, key requirements, and "DM or apply via link." Instagram and Facebook posts to be visually branded (use CKP hiring template if available).

⚠ Social media posts may generate direct messages — Yegi to monitor and redirect applicants to formal portal or email submission.

Social posts live within 1 working day of portal posting.
→
STEP 4C
Engage Recruitment Agencies
🤝

Yegi sends job brief to CKP's retained recruitment agencies: Intellipro and Career Horizons. Brief includes: role title, JD summary, mandatory requirements, salary range, urgency level, and interview process outline.

Agencies to submit CVs directly to Yegi. Yegi manages all agency communication — HODs do not liaise with agencies directly.

Agency-submitted CVs go through the same screening criteria as direct applicants. No bypass of Persona Card requirements.

Brief sent to agencies same day as portal posting. Request CVs within 5 working days.
↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 5
Screen & Shortlist CVs
📄

Yegi consolidates all CVs from portals, social media, and agency submissions into a single tracking sheet. Screens against: SRF role requirements, mandatory outsourcing/agency background, Persona Card criteria (CKP-HR-RPC-001).

Prepares colour-coded shortlist Excel (as per CKP screening standard): Green = proceed, Amber = borderline/query, Red = reject with reason.

Minimum 3 shortlisted candidates per role before proceeding to interview. If fewer than 3 qualified candidates, extend ad duration or brief agencies again — do not lower the bar.

Auto-reject: commercial/in-house background only. No exceptions without Caroll's explicit approval.

Shortlist compiled and sent to HOD within 5 working days of CV collection close.
↓
HOD + Yegi
STEP 6
Round 1 Interview
💬

Yegi schedules and conducts Round 1 together with HOD. Format is flexible depending on candidate availability and urgency:

Virtual: Google Meet or Teams — suitable for out-of-state candidates or time-sensitive roles. Yegi to confirm platform with candidate in advance.

Physical: At CKP office — preferred for final-stage candidates and roles involving frequent client interaction.

Both interviewers assess against Persona Card criteria and complete scoring sheet (to be formalised — see Gaps). Yegi takes notes and documents key observations.

Round 1 scheduled within 5 working days of shortlist approval by HOD.
→
STEP 7
Round 1 Outcome
◆

Proceed to Round 2: HOD and Yegi aligned — candidate moves forward.
Reject: Yegi notifies candidate professionally within 3 working days. Reason documented internally. Next shortlisted candidate called up.
Hold: Candidate parked pending other interviews completing — Yegi to manage timeline carefully.

Round 1 decision communicated to Yegi by HOD within 1 working day of interview.
↓
Caroll / Jeremy
STEP 8
Round 2 Interview
🎯

All candidates go through a second round of interview — this is standard for all hires at CKP, not just senior roles.

Staff / Executive level: Round 2 with Caroll (GM). Format: virtual or physical depending on schedule.

Manager level and above: Round 2 with Caroll, and Jeremy makes the final call.

Round 2 focuses on culture fit, values alignment, communication style, and long-term potential within CKP. Yegi attends to observe and take notes.

Round 2 scheduled within 5 working days of Round 1 clearance.
→
STEP 9
Round 2 Outcome
◆

Approved to proceed: Yegi moves to reference check.
Rejected: Return to shortlist. If no suitable candidates remain, re-open sourcing.
Undecided: Additional round or assessment may be requested — Caroll's call.

↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 10
Reference Check
🔎

Yegi conducts reference check on the selected candidate before any offer is issued. Minimum 2 references required — preferably former direct supervisors, not peers or personal contacts.

Reference check covers: employment dates and role confirmation, work performance and reliability, reason for leaving, conduct and professionalism, would the referee rehire the candidate?

Yegi documents all reference responses in writing and flags any concerns to Caroll before proceeding.

Negative or evasive references must be escalated to Caroll. Do not proceed to offer without Caroll's sign-off if concerns exist.

Reference check completed within 3 working days of Round 2 clearance. Do not issue offer until references are cleared.
↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 11
Prepare & Issue Offer Letter
📩

Yegi prepares conditional offer letter. Salary confirmed against approved SRF band. Letter includes: role title, salary, reporting line, proposed joining date, probation period (3 months standard), and a clear condition — offer is subject to satisfactory health screening.

No verbal offers. All offers in writing only. Offer letter must explicitly state the health screening condition.

Offer issued within 2 working days of reference check clearance. Candidate given 3 working days to accept.
→
STEP 12
Offer Accepted?
◆

Yes: Candidate signs and returns offer letter. Yegi proceeds to health screening arrangement.
Counter / Negotiation: Escalate to Caroll. Any salary adjustment requires Caroll approval before Yegi revises offer.
No: Yegi documents outcome and returns to shortlist or restarts sourcing.

Signed acceptance required in writing before health screening is arranged.
↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 13
Arrange Health Screening
🏥

Yegi issues health screening referral letter and arranges appointment at CKP's designated panel clinic: Gleneagles.

Standard pre-employment health screening covers: full blood count, liver and kidney function, blood glucose, chest X-ray, urine FEME, and any role-specific requirements.

Cost borne by CKP. Yegi to provide candidate with clinic details, referral letter, and date/time confirmation.

Health screening arranged within 3 working days of offer acceptance. Results expected within 5–7 working days from screening date.
→
STEP 14
Health Screening Result
◆

FIT — All clear: Yegi confirms joining date with candidate. Triggers Onboarding process (HR-PROC-002). HOD informed.

UNFIT — Fail: Offer is null and void. Yegi notifies candidate in writing citing health screening outcome. Caroll informed. Yegi returns to shortlist or restarts sourcing.

BORDERLINE — Requires review: Escalate to Caroll. Medical opinion may be sought before final decision. Do not confirm joining date until resolved.

Offer null and void on health screening failure — this is non-negotiable and must be stated clearly in the offer letter.

Decision communicated to candidate within 2 working days of receiving screening results.
↓
Yegi (HR)
STEP 15
Confirm Joining & Trigger Onboarding
🚀

Health screening passed. Yegi sends written confirmation of joining date to candidate. Notifies HOD and William (IT) of incoming hire. Closes recruitment record — documents full cycle in HR tracking file: source channel, agency (if any), rounds completed, reference check outcome, health screening result.

Formally triggers HR-PROC-002 (Onboarding) — pre-joining checklist begins D-5 from joining date.

Joining confirmation issued within 2 working days of health screening clearance.

Owner: Yegi Approver: Caroll (GM) Submission Deadline: 25th of each month System: HR Easily Statutory: EPF by 15th, SOCSO/EIS by 15th, PCB by 15th (following month)

📥

OT: verify against approved OT forms before keying. RM30 meal allowance and Grab fare per CKP OT policy.

All inputs collected by Day 18 of payroll month.

⌨️

STEP 3 · Day 20

🖥️

Yegi runs the payroll simulation (draft run) in HR Easily. System calculates: gross pay, EPF (employee + employer), SOCSO, EIS, PCB, net pay per employee.

Yegi exports payroll summary report and checks: total headcount matches, gross pay variance vs prior month flagged, any nil/zero pay line items.

Simulation completed by Day 20.

◆

Yegi reviews: any employee with variance >10% vs prior month — investigate cause. Any new/departed employee — confirm proration calculation. EPF/SOCSO rates correct for each salary band.

Extracted within 3 working days of payslip distribution.
→
STEP 2
Compare Actuals vs Budget
◆

Yegi compares actual staff costs against approved FY budget: total payroll vs budgeted payroll, headcount vs approved headcount, OT spend vs OT budget by department.

Within budget: Note and include in monthly HR report.
Variance >5%: Flag to Caroll with cause.
Variance >10%: Urgent escalation — Caroll + Jillian (Finance) same day.

Review completed within 1 working day of data extraction.

Yegi · Monthly
STEP 3

⏱️

Unapproved OT in payroll — escalate to HOD and Caroll immediately.

→
STEP 4

🎯

HR tracks whether individual staff productivity (billable/chargeable hours) meets targets set per role. Staff cost per hour vs billable output per hour. Flags to HOD and Caroll where cost is disproportionate to output.

⚠ Time cost targets per role to be set by Caroll with each HOD. HR monitors against agreed benchmarks once established.

Quarterly productivity cost review presented to Caroll and relevant HOD.
↓
Caroll · Quarterly

📋

Quarterly pack ready by 5th working day of new quarter: July, October, January, April.

HR-PROC-011 — Disciplinary Process

Defined escalation path for all disciplinary matters. Verbal warning through to show cause and termination. Consistent application across all departments with full documentation at every stage.

Owner: Yegi (documentation) + HOD (initiation) + Caroll (escalation) Legal ref: Employment Act 1955, Industrial Relations Act 1967 Principle: Document everything. No surprises at termination.

Click any step card to expand full detail.

HOD
STEP 1
Identify & Document Issue
⚠️

HOD identifies a disciplinary or performance concern (misconduct or persistent performance failure). Documents: date, nature of issue, evidence, prior verbal reminders. Submits to Yegi before any formal action is taken.

HODs must not issue any written warning or letter independently — all formal disciplinary documents go through Yegi and are approved by Caroll.

↓
Yegi + Caroll
STEP 2
Assess Severity — Which Level?
◆

Yegi and Caroll determine correct entry point:

Level 1 — Verbal Warning: First minor offence.
Level 2 — Written Warning: Repeated minor offence or moderate misconduct.
Level 3 — Show Cause: Serious or repeated misconduct requiring formal explanation.
Level 4 — Termination: Gross misconduct (theft, fraud, violence, harassment) — can bypass earlier levels.

When in doubt, start lower and document — do not jump to termination without a paper trail.

↓
Yegi
STEP 3A · Level 1
Verbal Warning
🗣️

HOD delivers verbal warning in private. Yegi prepares internal memo recording: date, who present, issue discussed, expected behaviour, employee's response. Filed in HR record. If issue recurs within 3 months, escalate to Written Warning.

Memo filed within 2 working days of verbal warning.
→
STEP 3B · Level 2
Written Warning Letter
📝

Yegi prepares written warning letter, approved by Caroll. States: specific issue, dates, prior verbal warning reference, corrective action required, consequence of recurrence. Employee signs acknowledgement of receipt. Copy filed, copy to employee.

Employee refuses to sign — Yegi notes refusal in writing with a witness. File stands regardless.

Letter issued within 5 working days of incident confirmation.
→
STEP 3C · Level 3
Show Cause Letter
⚖️

Yegi prepares show cause letter, approved by Caroll. States allegation(s) and requires written explanation within 5 working days. Caroll reviews response: accept (no further action), issue final warning, proceed to domestic inquiry, or termination.

Show cause is legally significant — do not deviate from procedure. Consult an employment lawyer for complex cases.

Employee: 5 working days to respond. Caroll decision: within 5 working days of response.
↓
Caroll · Level 4
STEP 4 · Level 4
Termination
🔴

Termination only after proper documentation trail (except gross misconduct). Caroll makes final decision. Yegi prepares termination letter: grounds, effective date, final pay entitlements. For gross misconduct, employee may be suspended with pay pending inquiry. Jeremy informed on all terminations. Yegi triggers HR-PROC-005 (Offboarding) immediately.

Wrongful dismissal claims are costly and damaging. The documentation trail from Step 1 is the only protection. Never terminate without it.

→
STEP 5 · All Levels
File & Monitor
📁

All disciplinary records filed in employee HR file immediately after each action. Yegi tracks open matters in HR Monthly Report: issue type, level, date of action, status, next review date. No disciplinary matter should be "pending" more than 30 days without a defined next step.
